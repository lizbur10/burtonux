AppledoreBanding.org

Requirements gathering, information architecture, style tiles

The Project

As a freelance project, designed (and developed) a dedicated website for the Appledore Island Migration Station on Appledore Island, Maine.

The Problem

The banding station's online presence was limited to a couple of faculty pages on a university website. As a result, the information was difficult to locate and difficult for banding station staff to keep updated. Furthermore, only scheduling and logistical information for volunteers was available on the site; the station had no public-facing online presence that might serve to attract potential new volunteers and supporters.

The Approach

I started by interviewing the head bander to gather information about her goals for the new site and then expanded the research to include all banding station volunteers. I then sketched out a proposed site map, created several style tiles showing some options for the visual treatment of the site, and worked with the head bander to finalize the style tile. Finally, I developed the site and launched it for the Spring 2016 bird banding season. I currently act as the site's webmaster, and continue to gather feedback from users, add and revise content, and re-evaluate and refine the structure and organization of the site.

The Result

The features of the new website include:

  • engaging content to attract and retain site visitors, including images and daily updates of banding activity during banding seasons
  • general information about the station and the research being conducted
  • scheduling and logistical information for station volunteers
  • the use of Wordpress as a CMS to make it easy to add and update content

My Role

Solo project: I did all design and development work.
